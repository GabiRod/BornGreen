Life of an oak

Did you ever wonder, what exactly a tree can do for the environment? Trees are interesting for many reasons; they are not just trendy gifts. Once a seed has been planted, it has begun it's journey towards collaborating thousands of organisms. Trees are amongst the most experienced and efficient nature conservators on earth!

Let's take a look, at the life of an oak . . .


Cool, huh?

Here's another fun fact; It is believed that trees can communicate with each other through out their root systems and a network of fungi. Trees can share resources, so the mother trees can help their seedlings, and the exhange of nutrients and sugars can warn neighbour trees of danger as well. This system has been nicknamed 'the Wood Wide Web'. But it gets better!

There's one forester, Peter Wohllben, who is studying this "tree language" in the hope that we might understand trees and their relations even better. Imagine that, communicating with your own family of trees!
